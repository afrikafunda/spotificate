How It Works

1

Create a free Audd.io account

This app uses Audd.io to identify your offline songs. Audd.io listens to your audio files and matches them to a database of known tracks — returning the title, artist, and Spotify URI so we can add them to your playlist.

  • Go to audd.io and click Sign Up
  • Verify your email and log in to the Audd.io dashboard
  • Copy your API token from the dashboard home page
Free tier: Audd.io gives you the first 300 requests for free. Each song file you upload uses one recognition. After 300 you'll need to upgrade.
2

Prepare your local music files

Gather the offline audio files you want to convert into a Spotify playlist. These are files sitting on your computer — downloaded songs, ripped CDs, voice memos, anything. The better the audio quality, the more accurate the recognition.

Supported formats:

.mp3 .wav .flac .aac .ogg .m4a .wma .aiff
Tip: If a song is not recognized it usually means the audio quality is too low, the file is heavily edited, or the track is too obscure for Audd.io's database. Live recordings and remixes are often harder to match.
3

Log in and paste your Audd.io key

Click Get Started, log in with your Spotify account, and you'll land on the dashboard. Head to the ⚙ Settings tab and paste your Audd.io API key there.

  • The key is stored only for your current session
  • It is never saved to any database
  • You will need to paste it again if you log out or close the browser
4

Create your playlist

Go to the Create Playlist tab. Give your playlist a name, then drag and drop your audio files into the upload area (or click to browse). Hit Recognize & Create and watch the progress live as each song gets identified and added to Spotify.

  • The playlist is created as private on your Spotify account
  • Songs that couldn't be matched are shown separately
  • Successfully created playlists are saved to your History tab for future restoration
